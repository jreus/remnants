In Search of Good Ancestors / Ahnen in Arbeit

A work in progress commissioned by CTM Festival, Deutschlandfunk Kultur & ÖRF Kunstradio

A year-long radio broadcast, artistic research process and series of workshops

  • How can data driven machine learning be explored as a form of unstable memory? Specifically: working through the materiality of generative voice and text AI?
  • How to use this materiality to reflect on data+models as cultural objects, subverting fixity and singularity?
  • How can an "ancestral cosmology" create artistic interventions around AI data governance?
Initial Conditions

"Will future generations speak of the wisdom of their ancestors as we are inclined to speak of ours?

It is relevant and even part of wisdom to ask not only are we being good citizens of the world today, but are we being good ancestors."

Jonas Salk, Acceptance Address: Jawaharlal Nehru Award for International Understanding (1977)

image: Jonas Salk, administering polio vaccine during early trials, 1956, source Wikimedia Commons
Initial Conditions: Text Prediction
  • BroadCaster models attempt to produce the likely what "should come next" from fragments of Jonas Salk's 1977 address
  • Text and Voice models start pre-trained on the most widely used ( "normative" ) open voice and text datasets
  • Text prompts and post-processing according to poetic rules and "magic word" annotations

Radio Stream recording February 12, 2022, a few days after broadcast launch

Initial Conditions: Voice Synthesis
  • prosody is stretched and warped during synthesis
  • synthesized voice is transformed into non-human and multi-human voice using real-time VAEs* and spectral morphing
  • goal to create perceptual uncertainty in excess of the "single" or "authoritative" voice

Radio Stream recording April 22, 2022, prior to second workshop

* RAVE variational autoencoder for fast and high-quality neural audio synthesis, Caillon and Esling 2021
